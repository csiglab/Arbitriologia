## Terminology

- **Technique** — A specific method, procedure, or skill used to accomplish a task.
- **Technique Space** — The set of all possible techniques available within a given domain; can be visualized as a landscape of options or capabilities.
- **Technical Change** — The process through which new techniques emerge, existing techniques are improved, or obsolete techniques are discarded.
- **Technique Evolution** — The dynamic transformation of the technique space over time, driven by technical change, experimentation, and innovation.
- **Technique Efficiency** — Evaluation of a technique based on performance metrics, resource usage, and effectiveness in achieving goals.
- **Technique Diffusion** — The spread and adoption of techniques across individuals, organizations, or societies.
- **Technique Interdependence** — The way techniques rely on, complement, or constrain each other within the technique space.
- **Technique Innovation Potential** — The capacity of a given technique or technique space to generate new, more effective methods.
- **Production Space** — The set of all feasible configurations, processes, and resources through which techniques can be applied to generate outputs. It represents the operational or systemic landscape in which techniques are enacted, including both physical and organizational dimensions.
- **Technical Reverse Engineering** — The systematic process of analyzing an existing technical system, product, or process to identify its underlying components, techniques, structures, and operational principles. It aims to understand how something works in order to replicate, improve, or integrate it into new designs.
- **Technical Replication** — The process of reproducing an existing technical system, product, or process by applying the same techniques, methods, and configurations identified in the original. It focuses on **faithful reproduction** rather than modification or innovation.
