## UK Industrial Policy

> Here is a comprehensive table summarizing key industrial policies in the UK from the 1500s to the present, highlighting major initiatives and their impacts:
> 

| **Policy/Act** | **Description** | **Impact and Notes** | **Period** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Statute of Artificers (1563) | Regulated apprenticeships and employment in crafts and trades | Established control over labor markets and skill development | 1500s |
| Navigation Acts (1651-1660) | Restricted colonial trade to English ships | Strengthened English maritime and commercial power | 1600s |
| Calico Acts (1700, 1721) | Banned import of printed cotton fabrics to protect the domestic textile industry | Encouraged growth of the British textile industry | 1700s |
| **Agricultural Reports** | Reports commissioned to study and recommend improvements in farming practices | Provided valuable data and recommendations for agricultural advancements | 1770s-1800s |
| **Introduction of New Crops and Techniques** | Promotion of crops like turnips and clover and techniques such as crop rotation and selective breeding | Improved soil fertility, crop yields, and livestock quality | 1800s |
| Corn Laws (1815-1846) | Imposed tariffs on imported grain to protect domestic agriculture | This led to the Anti-Corn Law League and its eventual repeal, promoting free trade | 1800s |
| Factory Acts (1833-1878) | Series of laws regulating working conditions in factories | Improved labor conditions, particularly for women and children | 1800s |
| National Insurance Act (1911) | Introduced social insurance for workers | An early form of social security and welfare | 1900s |
| Nationalization (1945-1951) | Post-war Labour government nationalized key industries (coal, steel, rail) | Aimed to rebuild and control essential industries | 1940s |
| Industrial Reorganization Corporation (1966) | Promoted mergers and efficiency in key industries | Focused on modernizing and consolidating industries | 1960s |
| Privatization (1980s) | Thatcher's government privatized many state-owned industries (telecom, energy) | Shift towards free market economy, reduced government intervention | 1980s |
| Enterprise Zones (1981) | Designated areas with tax incentives to encourage economic growth | Stimulated investment and job creation in targeted regions | 1990s |
| Science and Innovation Investment Framework (2004-2014) | Long-term strategy to increase R&D investment and innovation | Strengthened UK's position in global science and technology sectors | 2000s |
| Industrial Strategy (2017) | Focused on five foundations: ideas, people, infrastructure, business environment, and places | Aimed to boost productivity and ensure balanced regional growth | 2010s |
| UK Research and Development Roadmap (2020) | Plan to increase R&D investment to 2.4% of GDP by 2027 | Aims to position the UK as a global leader in science and technology | 2020s |

| **1785** | Act to Prevent the Exportation of Utensils Made Use of in the Cotton, Linen, Woollen, and Silk Manufactures of this Kingdom | It prohibited the export of machinery and tools used in textile manufacturing. | She maintained Britain's competitive edge in textile manufacturing. |
| **1799** | Exportation of Tools Act | She expanded the 1785 Act to include more types of machinery and tools used in various manufacturing processes. | It strengthened Britain's position as a leading industrial nation. |
