# Unit

> Research Entity

| **Aspect**                      | **Description**                                             | **Value(s)**                                                                             |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Role**                        | Primary function within the knowledge and innovation system | Basic research; Applied research; Translational; Policy advisory; Technology development |
| **Strategic Orientation**       | Dominant research posture                                   | Exploratory; Exploitative; Mission-oriented; Agenda-setting                              |
| **Position in Value Chain**     | Location in the knowledge-to-use pipeline                   | Upstream; Midstream; Downstream; End-to-end                                              |
| **Time Horizon**                | Typical temporal scope of research efforts                  | Short-term; Medium-term; Long-term                                                       |
| **Research Structure**          | Organizational form of research activity                    | Program-based; Project-based; PI-centric; Platform-based                                 |
| **Operational Workflow**        | How research progresses from question to output             | Linear; Iterative; Agile; Stage-gated                                                    |
| **Coordination Mechanism**      | How activities are synchronized                             | Hierarchical; Collegial; Modular; Networked                                              |
| **Human Capital Model**         | Composition and staffing logic                              | Permanent staff; Postdocs; Students; Visiting researchers                                |
| **Disciplinary Scope**          | Breadth of knowledge domains                                | Single-discipline; Multidisciplinary; Interdisciplinary; Transdisciplinary               |
| **Infrastructure Intensity**    | Dependence on physical or digital assets                    | Low; Medium; High                                                                        |
| **Funding Sources**             | Origin of financial resources                               | Core funding; Competitive grants; Contracts; Philanthropy; Commercial revenue            |
| **Funding Stability**           | Predictability of funding flows                             | Stable baseline; Mixed; Fully competitive                                                |
| **Cost Structure**              | Dominant expense profile                                    | Fixed-cost heavy; Variable-cost heavy                                                    |
| **Governance Structure**        | Formal authority arrangement                                | Director-led; Board-led; Collegial; Hybrid                                               |
| **Decision Rights**             | Who controls key decisions                                  | Centralized; Distributed; Shared                                                         |
| **Accountability Mechanisms**   | How performance is evaluated                                | Peer review; KPIs; External audits; Sponsor oversight                                    |
| **Autonomy Level**              | Degree of epistemic and managerial freedom                  | High; Medium; Low                                                                        |
| **Value Creation Type**         | Forms of value produced                                     | Epistemic; Economic; Societal; Symbolic                                                  |
| **Primary Beneficiaries**       | Main recipients of value                                    | Scientific community; Industry; State; Society                                           |
| **Value Capture Mechanisms**    | How value is retained or recognized                         | Publications; Patents; Spin-offs; Advisory influence                                     |
| **External Interfaces**         | Key interaction partners                                    | Universities; Firms; Government; NGOs                                                    |
| **Interaction Mode**            | Nature of engagement                                        | Open collaboration; Contractual; Embedded; Advisory                                      |
| **Knowledge Transfer Channels** | How knowledge leaves the unit                               | Journals; Data releases; Prototypes; Training                                            |
| **Boundary Management**         | Control of interfaces and risks                             | IP policies; Data governance; COI rules                                                  |
| **Adaptability**                | Capacity to reorient activities                             | High; Medium; Low                                                                        |
| **Systemic Role**               | Function in the wider system                                | Capability builder; Problem solver; Agenda setter                                        |

Research Entity Types:

- Laboratory
- Research Unit (Research Team)
- Research Center
- Institute
- Research Organization

| **Term**                                | **Description**                                                                                                         | **Where Used / Example**                                                       |
| --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Technology Transfer Office (TTO)**    | Institutional office that handles IP protection, licensing, and commercialization of university-generated technologies. | Common in universities worldwide (e.g., MIT TLO, Oxford University Innovation) |
| **Innovation Hub**                      | Multi-stakeholder space facilitating applied research, entrepreneurship, and commercialization.                         | MaRS (Canada), Innovation Hub (South Africa)                                   |
| **Translational Research Center**       | Focused on moving research “from bench to market” – often used in health, agri-food, or material science.               | Translational Health Science and Technology Institute (India)                  |
| **Technology Commercialization Center** | Specializes in market-readiness of R\&D outputs; often within larger research institutions.                             | Used by NASA, national labs, or defense innovation units                       |
| **Applied Innovation Institute**        | Research-performing organizations that co-develop solutions with industry for near-market deployment.                   | VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland                                       |
| **Living Lab**                          | Open innovation ecosystem where users, developers, and researchers co-create and test solutions in real-life settings.  | ENoLL (European Network of Living Labs)                                        |
| **Proof-of-Concept (PoC) Center**       | Focused on maturing early-stage research into validated prototypes or demonstrators.                                    | i-Capital, Oxford’s PoC funding programs                                       |
| **Industrial Innovation Center**        | Interfaces with firms to support R\&D-driven product and process development.                                           | Basque Culinary Center, Fraunhofer IAO                                         |
| **Technology Incubation Office**        | Supports new ventures and startups emerging from applied research.                                                      | Many in Asian universities (e.g., NUS, Tsinghua)                               |
